# Reward Economy Design Guide

## Balance Principles

### Early Game (First 1-2 hours)
- Rewards: Basic materials (iron, gold, food)
- Purpose: Teach mechanics, reduce grind
- Typical: 1-5 diamonds equivalent per quest
- Avoid: Giving too much too early (kills progression tension)

### Mid Game (2-10 hours)
- Rewards: Processed materials, machine parts, enchanted items
- Purpose: Accelerate boring grinds, reward clever play
- Typical: Mid-tier resources in moderate quantities
- Avoid: Rewards that trivialize the next chapter's challenges

### Late Game (10+ hours)
- Rewards: Rare items, creative-mode adjacent items, cosmetic items
- Purpose: Celebrate achievement, enable endgame builds
- Typical: Nether stars, dragon eggs, custom items
- Avoid: Game-breaking rewards (creative tab items)

## Reward Types by Purpose

### Progression Rewards (keep player moving)
- Small stacks of the NEXT chapter's key material
- Tools with useful enchantments
- XP for enchanting/repairing

### Convenience Rewards (reduce tedium)
- Storage upgrades (backpacks, drawers)
- Transport items (waystones, ender pearls)
- Automation components

### Celebration Rewards (mark milestones)
- Large diamond/emerald stacks
- Custom named items with lore text
- Choice rewards (let player pick what they need)
- Command rewards (for cosmetic effects)

### Cosmetic Rewards (endgame filler)
- Decorative blocks
- Banner patterns
- Custom heads

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Giving stack of diamonds in first quest (inflation)
- Random rewards with bad odds (frustration)
- Command rewards that can be abused on servers
- Rewards that bypass the progression you just designed
- Repeatable quests with high-value rewards (farming exploit)
